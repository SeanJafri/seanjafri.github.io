Active Sun Tracking Device

Sundog Engineering - UTARI

❮ ❯

The Sundog Engineering team was tasked by Dr. Kenneth Reifsnider of the UTA Research Institute (UTARI) to design and build a sun tracking device for solar powered streetlights. Our team succesfully created a two-axis positioning system for the solar panel, outfitted with an array of photocells, to accurately find the center position of the sun throughout the day. The positioning system orients itself using a linear actuator and a chain drive, controlled by a low power Arduino microcontroller, and can withstand normal environmental operating conditions. The device is capable of fully charging the battery, keeps the streetlight on all night (11+ hours), and rotates overnight to face the next sunrise.
